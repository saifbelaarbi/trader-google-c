No architecture makes a strategy profitable — it only makes profitability *measurable*. The
gates exist because the fastest way to lose the eventual real bankroll is to skip them.

## Go-live gates

All must pass before switching to real money:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enough evidence" icon="calendar-check">
    ≥ 4 weeks of dry-run **and** ≥ 30 closed trades.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Profitable and consistent" icon="chart-line">
    Dry-run profit factor ≥ **1.3**, win rate within ~10 points of backtest.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Survivable drawdown" icon="shield-halved">
    Max dry-run drawdown ≤ **15%** of wallet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="No unexplained divergence" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Backtest and dry-run behavior line up. Any gap is understood, not hand-waved.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## First live steps (when the gates pass)

<Warning>
  Start small and de-risked. This is the most dangerous moment in the whole plan.
</Warning>

- Live Bybit keys; start with **$50–100**, not the full intended capital.
- **Halve** position sizes for the first 2 weeks.
- **Tighten** the `MaxDrawdown` protection.
- Test **one** manual trade before enabling autonomous entries.
- Watch the first 5 live trades closely via Telegram.
- Secure keys via [Secret Manager](/run/gcp), enable Bybit IP allow-listing, disable
  withdrawals.

## Standing safety rules

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Never edit the live strategy mid-window" icon="lock">
    Changing `ClaudeBreakout` parameters or logic during the paper test invalidates the
    experiment. Tuning happens on [research variants](/research/variants) in backtests.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Never commit filled-in secrets" icon="key">
    `config.dry.json` keeps placeholders in the repo; your Telegram token, jwt, and password
    stay local (or in Secret Manager).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="One Telegram consumer per token" icon="paper-plane">
    Run the bot in exactly one place. Two instances on the same token cause update conflicts.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Dry-run flatters" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Real fills slip, funding drags, fees bite. The realism pass and the gates are precisely
    why backtest optimism doesn't become real losses.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="One tweak per week" icon="hand">
    No constant fiddling. Surviving losing streaks is the precondition for collecting on
    winning ones.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
