`ClaudeBreakout` (file: `ftbot/strategies/ClaudeBreakout.py`) is a turtle-style Donchian
breakout on 4h candles. It trades **with** the macro trend and lets winners run.

## Rules

<Steps>
  <Step title="Entry">
    Go **long** when the close breaks the prior **20-bar high** and price is **above
    EMA200**. Go **short** on a **20-bar low** break **below EMA200**. Volume must be > 0.
    The EMA200 filter means it only trades in the direction of the macro trend.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Exit">
    Close the position when price crosses the opposite **10-bar Donchian extreme**. There is
    no fixed take-profit — the channel itself is the trailing exit, giving multi-day trends
    room to run.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stop">
    A hard **2×ATR(14)** stop from entry (a disaster stop, not a profit manager). A
    `-20%` failsafe sits behind it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Position sizing

Each trade is a flat notional stake: `min($40, 20% of equity)`, leverage 1×. Risk-based
("unit") sizing — where every trade risks the same fraction of equity at its stop — is a
[research lever](/research/returns), not yet in the live strategy.

## Capital protection

Three freqtrade protections guard against losing streaks:

| Protection | Trigger | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `CooldownPeriod` | after any trade | pause that pair 1 candle |
| `StoplossGuard` | 4 stops in ~2 weeks | halt new entries 12 candles |
| `MaxDrawdown` | 15% drawdown over 30 days | halt new entries 42 candles |

## Expected profile

<Note>
  Judge on **profit factor** and **expectancy**, not win rate. Most trades are small losses;
  a few multi-ATR winners carry the book.
</Note>

- Win rate ~30%, winners average ~+5% over ~6 days, losers ~-4%.
- ~0.8 trades/day across 9 pairs.
- Drawdowns to ~26% and **losing streaks past 20 are within backtest norms** — not a
  malfunction.

## Robustness

The edge survives parameter changes (a real edge is a plateau, not a spike). All neighbors
backtest positive:

| Variant | Profit | PF | Max DD |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 20/10, EMA200 (default, live) | +40.9% | 1.15 | 25.9% |
| Fast 15/8 | +40.1% | 1.13 | 29.2% |
| Slow 30/15 | +73.8% | 1.31 | 22.7% |
| EMA150 | +37.2% | 1.13 | 29.6% |
| EMA250 | +63.0% | 1.25 | 20.3% |

<Tip>
  The slow gradient (30/15, EMA250) is clearly better in-sample, but the **default was
  pre-registered** to avoid selection bias. Adopt the slow region only via
  [walk-forward hyperopt](/research/backtesting), never by grabbing the prettiest number.
</Tip>
